yahkāskwan mīhkiwap

(light pole* tipi)

Many years ago I had a vision where I saw a tipi made of light.

After many years following of thinking, experimenting and researching equipment and materials, I’ve created a performative and participatory event using accessible modern technology and ceremonial earth medicines. 15 Handheld spot beams (800 lumens each) and multiple sage smudge sticks/bundles are what are used to manifest this tipi. The main difference with this tipi-raising is that it engages people who together, create and become the poles of a tipi and thus is a community-engaged activity. It is participatory action, group visualization and what I like to think of as an example of Indigenous or NDN VR (virtual reality).

To date, the light tipi has made appearances worldwide:

Thanks to Joseph Naytowhow for his ongoing assistance in this project.

* yahkāskwan actually refers to 'lightness' in weight and not lumens

40 Blocks

Forty Blocks (1994) is a music video which chronicles a Metis woman's journey from a home, where as a child she was abused, across town to her Kokum's house where she was never allowed to go. The journey and the song it inspires are healing, as she reclaims her connection to her culture, her blood and the earth. "this dance on earth, my lifelong search, a right to passage within the arms of my Kokom."

Directed, written and filmed by Cheryl L'Hirondelle

Produced by Sharon Stevens and Kathy Dodd

distributed by vtape.org
